What is an EMI Calculator?

EMI = Equated Monthly Installment. It's the fixed amount you pay every month to repay a loan over a set period.

EMI Formula

EMI = P × r × (1+r)ⁿ / ((1+r)ⁿ − 1)

Where P = principal, r = monthly interest rate, n = number of months.

Tips to reduce your EMI

  • Make a larger down payment to reduce the principal
  • Choose a longer tenure (though you'll pay more interest)
  • Negotiate a lower interest rate with your lender

What is BMI?

Body Mass Index (BMI) is a measure calculated from your height and weight. Formula: BMI = weight(kg) ÷ height(m)²

BMI limitations

  • Doesn't distinguish between muscle and fat
  • Athletes may have high BMI but low body fat
  • Always consult a doctor for health decisions

What is BMR?

Basal Metabolic Rate (BMR) is the number of calories your body needs at rest. TDEE multiplies BMR by your activity level to give total daily calorie needs.

Formula used (Mifflin-St Jeor)

Male: (10 × kg) + (6.25 × cm) − (5 × age) + 5
Female: (10 × kg) + (6.25 × cm) − (5 × age) − 161

Frequently Asked Questions

How do I calculate overtime pay?

Overtime pay = Overtime Hours × (Rate × 1.5). Under US federal law, overtime applies after 40 hours/week. Use our free Timecard Calculator above to calculate it automatically.

How is EMI calculated?

EMI = P × r × (1+r)^n / ((1+r)^n−1). Where P = principal, r = monthly rate, n = months. Our EMI Calculator computes this instantly with a full amortization schedule.

How do I convert hourly to annual salary?

Annual salary = Hourly Rate × Hours/week × 52. Example: $20/hr × 40 hrs × 52 = $41,600/year. Use our Salary Calculator for instant conversions with net pay after tax.

What is BMI and how is it calculated?

BMI = weight (kg) ÷ height (m)². Under 18.5 = underweight, 18.5–24.9 = normal, 25–29.9 = overweight, 30+ = obese. Try our free BMI Calculator above.

Is Calcuzza completely free?

Yes — all calculators on Calcuzza are 100% free with no signup, no subscription, and no ads. Everything runs in your browser.

What is compound interest?

Compound interest grows your money on both the principal AND previously earned interest. Formula: A = P(1+r/n)^(nt). Use our Compound Interest Calculator for year-by-year projections.
